Interpretation of our results:

(1) --> Increasing study hours leads to an average improvement of about 0.13 standard deviation 
        units in student performance, indicating a positive impact.

(2) --> The p-value of 0.028 shows that the observed effect is statistically significant, 
        meaning it is unlikely to have occurred by chance.

(3) --> The confidence interval [0.014, 0.246] suggests that the true effect is positive and 
        likely falls within this range, reinforcing the reliability of the result.

(4) --> The typical (median) student in the control group 
        performs slightly better than in the treated group.

Overall Insight:
There is strong evidence that higher study hours have a positive and 
statistically significant effect on student performance, particularly for the dataset we've chosen.

Chart Explanation - 

Median (middle line in the boxes):
The median line represents the typical performance. Although the treated group shows some skewness 
due to low-performing outliers, its median is still slightly higher than the control group 
if we disregard the outliers, indicating better average performance.

Box (middle 50% of students):
The treated group (high study hours) has a wider box, 
showing more variation in performance among students who study more.

Outliers (dots):
Outliers in the treated group represent students who performed poorly despite studying more.
This suggests that studying more does not guarantee success for every student.
