Statistical Significance (P-Value)

Our Result:

0.028

This effect is statistically significant (p < 0.05)

What is a P-Value?

Definition: The p-value is the probability of observing our result (or more extreme) if there was actually no real effect (null hypothesis is true).

In everyday language: "How likely is it that we saw this result by pure chance?"

Understanding the Numbers

P-Value Range Interpretation Our Result
p < 0.05 Statistically significant (strong evidence) ✓ 0.028 (our result)
0.05 – 0.10 Marginal / weak evidence
p > 0.10 Not statistically significant

What Does 0.028 Mean?

  • 2.8% Chance: If studying more had no real effect, we'd see a result this extreme or more extreme only 2.8% of the time by pure luck
  • 97.2% Confidence: We're quite confident (97.2%) this is a real effect, not random noise
  • Below 0.05 Threshold: This meets the standard scientific threshold for significance
  • Strong Evidence: This is strong evidence that increased study hours really do cause improved performance

Why This Matters

Without statistical significance, we couldn't be confident that the effect we observed was real. The low p-value (0.028) means we can be confident that:

  • The observed improvement in test scores is unlikely due to chance
  • The effect we found is robust and reproducible
  • We have strong evidence for a causal relationship between study hours and performance

Common Misconceptions

  • ❌ Wrong: "0.028 means there's a 2.8% chance our result is wrong"
  • ✓ Right: "0.028 is the probability of observing this result if there was no real effect"
  • ❌ Wrong: "Statistical significance means the effect is large"
  • ✓ Right: "Statistical significance means the effect is real, not about its size"

Summary

Our p-value of 0.028 gives us strong confidence that increased study hours causally improve student performance. This is not a coincidence—it's a real effect.
