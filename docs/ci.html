What is a Confidence Interval?

A 95% Confidence Interval (CI) is a range of values that we're 95% confident contains the true effect in the population.

Important: The true effect is the actual impact of study hours on student performance in the entire population of students, not just the dataset.

Understanding [0.014, 0.246]

  • Lower Bound (0.014): The minimum likely effect. Even in the most conservative scenario, studying more likely improves performance by at least 0.014 SDs
  • Upper Bound (0.246): The maximum likely effect. The benefit is unlikely to exceed 0.246 SDs
  • Point Estimate (0.13): Our best single estimate of the true effect—the midpoint of the range

Why This Matters

CI Interpretation What It Means Our Case?
Does NOT cross 0 The effect is likely real (positive or negative) ✓ [0.014, 0.246] is entirely above 0
Wide interval Lots of uncertainty about the true effect ✗ Not our case
Crosses 0 Effect could be positive or negative (not significant) ✗ Not our case

What This Tells Us

Because our 95% CI is [0.014, 0.246] (entirely above zero) we know:

  • ✅ Positive Effect Confirmed: Increased study hours genuinely improve performance
  • ✅ Effect is Real: Not due to mathematical chance
  • ✅ Reasonable Precision: We're not wildly uncertain—the interval is fairly tight
  • ✅ Practical Insight: The true effect is somewhere between a small and small-to-moderate improvement

Connecting the Pieces

  • Point Estimate (ATE): 0.13 — our best single guess
  • P-Value: 0.028 — confirms this effect is real, not random
  • 95% CI: [0.014, 0.246] — the likely range of the true effect

Together, these tell us: "Increased study hours have a real, positive effect on performance, and it's likely to be between 0.014 and 0.246 standard deviations."

Summary

Our 95% confidence interval of [0.014, 0.246] is entirely above zero, providing strong evidence that increasing study hours causally improves student performance.
